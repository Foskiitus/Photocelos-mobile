Heraldry

The Heraldic symbols Parish Carapeços consisting of Arms, Flag and Seal were chosen and approved by the Parish Council, chaired by Francisco de Assis Real Tomé at a special meeting of the Executive Board, held on 20 February 2001. The choice was made from among several proposals submitted by the company's specialty, Diácria Editora Lda. whom the work of sorting, which was based on the study and interpretation of the known history of the parish had been ordered. That same day, February 20, 2001, and after appropriate approval, the symbols were sent to the Heraldry Committee of Portuguese Archaeologists, for issuance of its opinion in accordance with Law No. 53/91 of 7 August.
This opinion, favorable, was issued twenty months later, on 7 October 2002 and the Parish Assembly Carapeços, held that mandatory opinion finally approved the establishment of the heraldic symbols of the parish Carapeços, at a meeting on 26 December 2002 proposal by following the ordering and the opinion received from the previous Board, and sent by this now chaired by Bartolomeu Correia Batista Barbosa made ​​under subparagraph q) of paragraph 2 of Article 17 of Law No. 169/99, of September 18, thus institutionally and making the public.
The research work done by sorting and Diácria Editora and produced according to very strict rules of law regarding the provision of symbology and number of eligible colors had collaboration at the first meeting place, and as guests of the Board, Messrs. P. Alcino da Cunha Pereira and Manuel da Costa Pereira who gave their opinions to the proposed work. The remaining meetings were held with the Board during the late 2000 and early 2001.
