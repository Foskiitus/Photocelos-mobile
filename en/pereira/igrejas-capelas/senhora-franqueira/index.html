Shrine of Our Lady of Franqueira

The foundation of the small church dedicated to Our Lady of Franqueira is traditionally attributed to Egas Moniz, although the oldest architectural elements of the current structure of the temple date back to the fifteenth century.
The flatness of the temple would be quite transformed over the seventeenth and eighteenth centuries, increasing the volume of the building and significantly changing the facade.
The primitive structure of the hermitage corresponds to rectangular body of the chancel, which preserves the primitive Romanesque cornice taste and the Gothic ribbed vault. Inside is an altar brought from Ceuta by Afonso, Count of Barcelos around 1415. The space ship was built later, perhaps as early as the seventeenth century, since the door of the sacristy presents the data recorded in 1691.
The façade presents a model of vertical lines whose decorative program makes the transition between the floor and the baroque style taste. The portal, simple rectangular frame is flanked by two janelos and surmounted by a large bay window with iron guard. The set is topped by a triangular pediment with a niche that houses the statue of the Virgin, and on the right the tower was built in 1753 by Pedro Gomes Simões.
