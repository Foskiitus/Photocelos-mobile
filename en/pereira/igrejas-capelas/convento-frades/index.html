Church of the Convent of Frades

Quinta do Convento da Franqueira was built in mid-1560, with stones taken from the ruins of a castle situated in its vicinity, the Castelo de Faria. This castle was during the fourteenth century fortress D. Nuno Gonçalves, Alcaide-mor of the castle during the reign of King Ferdinand (1367-1383). Taken prisoner by the Spaniards, was taken to the walls of Castelo de Faria, in an attempt to convince his son, Gonçalo Nunes, then chief of garrison to surrender. However Nuno Gonçalves took the opportunity to urge his son to resistance, so it was promptly shot down. The Franciscan friars were attracted to the place by the former east, the 'Fountain of Life', known for its curative powers since 1493. The friars remained in Franqueira until the Dissolution of the Monasteries in 1834, the year the convent and its grounds were auctioned State and sold to a family of Porto. When in 1965 a naval officer retired English and his wife decide to purchase the convent, this was already in an advanced state of degradation. From there, it began the process of restoration and conservation project that now continues with his son, Piers Gallie.
