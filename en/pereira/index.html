Pereira

Parish in the municipality of Barcelos, Braga District.
This crater is 6 km from the county seat, and is situated approximately 3 km south of the left bank of the River Trough.
Its patron is Divine Savior.
Inquiries in the D. Afonso II of 1220 it is said that this "Vila Honored" Ensign belongs to Pedro Pais.
The Parish Church was originally on white ground, place the Cabacinha. By sec. XVII, was moved to where it is today. It was a building of modest proportions.
Foi restaurada e ampliada em 1975.
To the west of the parish stands the Mount Franqueira. There at the top, this Shrine of Our Lady of Franqueira, whose foundation is attributed to Egas Monis (1200).
Began as a small hermitage, reduced will present chancel retains the cornice of this romantic traits, but the vault is Gothic.
What was the main altar and the three pillars that sustains it, are of jasper: an offering to Our Lady that Duke Alfonso, 8th Count of Barcelos, brought Ceuta, in the time of King John I in 1415 .
The body of the shrine is much more recent construction.
From the top of Mount Franqueira glimpses a beautiful panorama.
Descending the northern slope, we can admire the ruins of the Castle of Faria.
A little further down, draws attention today commonly called the Convent of the Friars.
In 1429, two spouses, Porto, decided to dispose of their goods and raise ai a poor houses and a chapel with the title of "Bom Jesus da Franqueira" in the place where now is the enclosure of the convent.
In these early hermits, later joined the Padres Cloistered or Castilian.
In 1505 was to belong to the Franciscan Friars, by the decree of the convent, put one surmounted by the image of Jesus Crucified to signify that he is the Source of Life Source, the Convent Church today still is called the Church of the Lord of the Fountain of Life .
For the construction of the present convent, took advantage of the stones of the historic Castle Faria, near here
The yard of the Church of the Convent down a staircase that ends well underway with two small chapels with images relating to the passion of the Lord.
More like chapels flanking an ancient road that descended in the extension of that staircase.
The Chapel of Nossa Senhora da Guia in Silgueiros Place, was built in 1747 by Manuel Fernandes Braziela, born in Pereira, Away in Brazil.
Has characteristics of the Renaissance.
Began to be called the Chapel of the Fountain of Cidral and was dedicated to the souls in purgatory, as well witness the artistic reredos over the altar.
In 1959 was under private management Lucinda Fernandes da Silva, who donated to the parish.
Public their sources are: of the Entre Devesas, Amingua, Varziela, Silgueiros e Cidral (This shaped fountain, falling water through a spout into a stone bowl)
